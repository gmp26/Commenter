A quick guide to markdown

Adding emphasis

*italic*   **bold**
  _italic_   __bold__

Include mathematics using `LaTeX`

`LaTeX` maths should be bracketed by single `\$` signs if it is to appear inline, or by double `\$\$` signs to appear centered and separate. 

  Straight lines have equations of the form $y=ax+b$, 
  but other forms are quite often useful. 

  For example $$ \frac{y-3}{x-2} = 6 $$ will give
  you a line of gradient $6$ that goes through 
  the point $(2,3)$.

Headers

  ========

  Header 2
  --------

or

# Header 1
  ## Header 2
  ### Header 3
  ...
  ###### Header 6

Lists

Ordered, without paragraphs:

1.  Foo
  2.  Bar

Unordered, with paragraphs:

*   A list item.

  With multiple paragraphs.

  *   Bar

You can nest lists:

*   Abacus
    * answer
  *   Bubbles
    1.  bunk
    2.  bupkis
      * BELITTLER
    3. burper
  *   Cunning

<code> spans are delimited by backticks.

You can include literal backticks like `this` .
